Plant | Chromatin immunoprecipitation

2021-04-16 | Plant

##Crosslinking

  1. Prepare crosslinking buffer (Extraction buffer 1). For each gram of leaves, 36 ml buffer is needed.
  2. Prepare 2M glycine solution (for stopping the crosslinking reaction). For each gram of leaves, 2.5 ml solution is needed. Store at 4°C.
  3. Collect beaker with appropriate capacity. Make sure the buffer volume will not exceed 60-70% of the beaker capacity (to avoid spillage).
  4. Harvest soybean leaves (C08).
  5. Cut leaves into 0.5-1 cm pieces and submerge them in Milli-Q water in beaker.
  6. Rinse leaves with Milli-Q water twice.
  7. Add 5 mM beta-ME into the cold extraction buffer 1. Pour buffer into the beaker with leaves.
  8. Place the beaker into the transparent “bowl” for vacuum infiltration.
  9. Disconnect centrivap with the cold trap.
  10. Connect the bowl with the cold trap.
  11. Add appropriate volume of formaldehyde solution to the buffer. 1 ml formaldehyde solution in 36 ml extraction buffer 1. working concentration ~ 1% formaldehyde
  12. Stir the mixture with stirrer.
  13. Place a piece of gauze under the bottom of a flask/beaker.
  14. Place the flask/beaker with gauze onto the water surface in the beaker.
  15. Cover the lid of the “bowl”.
  16. Close the valve at the cold trap. (Turn the valve to a position perpendicular to the pipe).
  17. Turn on the vacuum pump. After a while, the lid will be closed tightly and bubbles can be observed in the solution. Vacuum infiltrate for 5 minutes.
  18. Turn off the pump. Open the valve and the lid.
  19. Stir the solution again and make sure all leaves are submerged. Repeat step 16-18.
  20. Add appropriate volume of 2M glycine solution to the buffer. For each gram of leaves, 2.5 ml solution is added.
  21. Vacuum infiltrate for 5 minutes again.
  22. Cover the opening with the gauze already used in vacuum infiltration and filter out all the buffer under running tap water in a sink.
  23. Rinse the cross-linked leaves with Milli-Q water. (Cold Milli-Q water is preferred in some protocols.)
  24. With the aid of a stirrer/rod, transfer cross-linked leaves onto layers of tissue paper.
  25. Remove excess water by gently pressing cross-linked leaves with tissue paper.
  26. Pack leaves with aluminum foil.
  27. Freeze leaves with liquid nitrogen. Store at -80°C.
